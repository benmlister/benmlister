Project: Smart hot water

With the cost of living increasing year over year, it is becoming more and more difficult to try and save money. Energy prices are ever increasing, water is becoming more and more valuable due to its short supply. Electric hot water systems are unfortunately not the most efficient way to heat water and there is currently no way to manage the use of hot water in households. This isn’t surprising when electric hot water systems haven’t changed in design for at over 20 years. This project aims to create a Smart Electric Hot Water System that automatically adjusts its efficiency around your household and your day-to-day activities and calendar events.

The goal of this project is to create a Smart Electric Hot Water System that automatically adjusts the volume and temperature that water is heated to based on household water-use patterns and external factors like intake water temperature. For example, the system would, over a course of time, pick up on how many times a day people would have showers, at what times and the duration. It could potentially automatically create profiles for different family members based on the information it collects over time. The software could also identify changes in flow when other household appliances are using hot water.

This product will have several hardware and software features. No longer will hot water systems be a dull grey cylinder placed in a cupboard hidden from the world. This Smart Hot Water System will feature an elegant rectangular design, crafted out of recycled anodised aluminium. It is will be available in multiple colour options and available in both a gloss and matte finish. Built-in is a generous OLED touchscreen that displays daily, weekly and monthly water and electricity usage information, as well as providing a friendly user interface for the initial setup. This unit also employs the use of WiFi and Bluetooth LE to allow it to connect to mobile phones for an easier setup option, and also allows it to connect to home automation software. The WiFi connection also allows the Smart Hot Water System to receive software updates in an easy way. The unit features 100% serviceability and all parts will be recyclable, this will ensure a long service life from the device. Software will be what drives this system enabling its energy and water saving features. The software will use machine learning to run data through a model and identify water-use patterns allowing it to automatically adjust the volume of water, resulting in less energy being required. Other software features include the following:

  • Compatibility with home automation software, including HomeKit
  • Parental control to allow parents to set shower limits for shower-hogging teenagers
  • Cloud connect program that links in with your calendar to allow the hot water system to identify periods when the house might be empty, like when you’re on a vacation
  • Flexible API to allow third parties to extend capabilities
  • Automatically tariff identification. If your Smart Hot Water System is connected to an energy saving tariff, it will automatically identify how long it has power and automatically adjust the volume and temperature of the water to ensure that there is enough hot water throughout the day
Also available will be an Android and iOS/ipadOS app that allows you to make manual tweaks to the settings, for example when additional guests might be staying with you. The app will also allow you to set and receive usage alerts if you have exceeded your daily usage limit.

Tools and technologies to create the prototype and achieve the desired outcome will include using a Raspberry PI computer with a multitude of sensors such as multiple temperature sensors, ambient temperature sensor, water level sensor, power sensor. A WLAN and Bluetooth LE chip will also be required. Software will be developed in JAVA, as it is well documented, supports machine learning and is easy to compile across multiple operating systems.

Other useful skills to ensure a successful project will include a software engineer with expertise in JAVA, a thermodynamics specialist to work on correct insulation to prevent heat loss and contribute ideas to allow an efficient heating element, a mechanical and electrical engineer would be useful to work on product and circuitboard design. Later in the project, an app developer with sufficient experience with SWIFT and JAVA programming languages would be essential in creating an app for both the iOS and Android operating systems. A graphic designer that specialises in App design and user experience would be critical to ensure that the App and built-in software are easy and intuitive to use.

If this project is successful, a Smart Electric Hot Water System will be the outcome. It will allow thousands of people to conserve energy and water whilst saving money when it comes to hot water. It will allow users to become more familiar with their water usage allowing them to monitor and improve how their household uses hot water. A side benefit will also be that fewer hot water systems will end up in landfill due to the hot water system having 100% user replaceable parts and being 100% recyclable.
